Jul 12 1935
Return

Tracking down Benwick's essay has proved far more difficult than expected. A simple inquiry at the various libraries to which I have access has bore little fruit—indeed I struggle to find any works by this author. I suspect Dr. Grey's presence in the British Isles must be to blame—clearly no such works have made it to this side of the Atlantic.

My correspondence with Dr. Grey, however, has been far more insightful. She describes having observed similar phenomenon in Askrigg, and I am ever more assured of her experience. Her advice remains clear: no human—even I—should approach this strange aberration. She has similarly refused my offers to have samples I have taken delivered to her home, despite my offer to pay for all relevant expenses.

This cold-footedness remains a source of rather significant frustration, presenting no avenues to continue my research besides simply waiting to receive her next letter. Added to that the occasional difficulty of deciphering her style of prose, with its deviations and obtusions, and I find it difficult to remain patient.

I will spend some time further searching for Benwick's essay. I have a few friends on the Isles who may be able to help—and who shall at least be quicker to respond. Should my search still leave me wanting, I plan to resume my own investigations, regardless of Dr. Grey's warnings.

We shall see. Perhaps her next letter will hold some revelation to sate me.
